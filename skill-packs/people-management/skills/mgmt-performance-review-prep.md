name: performance-review-prep
description: "Gather evidence and structure a performance review for a direct report."
---

Produces an evidence-backed review prep document. Every claim has a source. Flags where data is thin rather than filling gaps with impressions.

## Context Loading

1. Read `sidekick/context/team-directory.md` - confirm the person's role, tenure, and focus areas
2. Read `sidekick/context/current-priorities.md` - understand what the team was working toward during this period
3. Read `sidekick/context/communication-style.md` - match tone for the delivery conversation
4. Search `sidekick/outputs/` for 1:1 docs, prior reviews, and feedback notes for this person

If the person isn't named, ask: "Who is this review for and what period does it cover?" before starting.

## Process

1. **Identify the person and period** - confirm from team-directory. Define the review window.
2. **Gather evidence** - search outputs/ for their name across 1:1 docs, meeting notes, decisions, and task records.
3. **Structure findings** - map evidence to accomplishments, growth, and development areas.
4. **Draft the review prep** - follow the output format below. Mark every point with its evidence source.
5. **Save output** - write to `sidekick/outputs/review-prep-[name]-[DATE].md`

## Output Format

```markdown
# Performance Review Prep: [Name]

**Period:** [start] to [end] | **Role:** [title] | **Prepared:** [DATE]

## Key Accomplishments

| Accomplishment  | Evidence                              | Impact                         |
| --------------- | ------------------------------------- | ------------------------------ |
| [What they did] | [Source: 1:1 doc, meeting note, etc.] | [Effect on team or priorities] |

## Areas of Growth

- [Skill or behavior that improved] — evidence: [source]

## Development Areas

- [Specific area to address] — evidence: [source] — suggested action: [what to do about it]

## Impact on Team Priorities

[How their work connected to the current-priorities.md goals during this period]

## Specific Examples

[2-3 concrete anecdotes to use in the conversation — positive and developmental]

## Data Gaps

[Areas where evidence was insufficient. Do not rate these — flag them for discussion.]

## Suggested Conversation Structure

1. [Opening — set tone]
2. [Strengths — lead with evidence]
3. [Development areas — SBI framing]
4. [Growth plan — forward-looking]
5. [Close — confirm shared understanding]
```

## Rules

- Every accomplishment and development area must cite an evidence source. No impressions.
- Mark data gaps explicitly. Do not fill thin evidence with guesses.
- Development areas are behavioral and specific, not character assessments.
- This document is for manager use only. It is not the review document itself.
