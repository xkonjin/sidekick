Produces a structured feedback plan for a specific conversation. Covers the SBI statement, opening, key points, questions, desired outcome, and follow-up. Works for both developmental and positive feedback.

## Context Loading

1. Read `sidekick/context/team-directory.md` - confirm the person's role and context
2. Read `sidekick/context/communication-style.md` - match the directness and tone appropriate for this relationship
3. Search `sidekick/outputs/` for prior 1:1 docs or review prep for this person - check for relevant history

If the topic isn't clear, ask: "What behavior or situation is this feedback about?" before starting.

## Process

1. **Identify the person and topic** - who is this for, and what specifically happened?
2. **Apply SBI** - map the situation, observable behavior, and its impact. Keep behavior factual, not interpretive.
3. **Anticipate their perspective** - what might they say? What context might they provide?
4. **Draft the session plan** - follow the output format below.
5. **Save output** - write to `sidekick/outputs/feedback-[name]-[DATE].md`

## Output Format

```markdown
# Feedback Session Prep: [Name]

**Date:** [DATE] | **Topic:** [brief description] | **Type:** Developmental / Positive / Mixed

## SBI Statement

**Situation:** [When and where — specific, not "you always"]
**Behavior:** [What they did or said — observable, not inferred]
**Impact:** [What happened as a result — on the team, project, or you]

## Suggested Opening

[1-2 sentences to set the tone and frame the conversation as a dialogue]

## Key Points to Make

1. [Core message — keep it to three points maximum]
2. [Second point]
3. [Third point]

## Questions to Ask

- [Question that invites their perspective on the situation]
- [Question about what they would do differently or what support they need]

## Desired Outcome

[What should be different after this conversation? Be specific.]

## Follow-Up Plan

| Action                    | Owner        | By when |
| ------------------------- | ------------ | ------- |
| [Specific follow-through] | [You / Them] | [Date]  |
```

## Rules

- Behavior only — never describe character or intent. "You seemed disengaged" is not observable. "You didn't respond to three Slack messages" is.
- Include the other person's perspective. Feedback is a dialogue, not a verdict.
- Maximum three key points. More than that and nothing lands.
- Never send or share this document externally. It is planning material only.
- Positive feedback follows the same structure — specificity matters either way.
